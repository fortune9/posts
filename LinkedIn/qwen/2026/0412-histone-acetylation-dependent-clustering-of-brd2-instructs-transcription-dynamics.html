Nature Genetics — BRD2 maintains Pol II recruitment at promoters through TFIID interaction, critical under impaired pause release

A study in Nature Genetics examined how BET proteins regulate transcription. In mouse embryonic stem cells:

  • BRD2–TFIID interaction — BRD2 maintains Pol II recruitment at promoters, particularly when pause release is impaired
  • H4 acetylation drives clustering — MOF-mediated histone H4 acetylation promotes BRD2 chromatin association and enables BRD2 clustering
  • IDR deletion recapitulates defects — Deleting BRD2's intrinsically disordered region mirrors the transcription machinery defects seen upon BRD2 loss

These findings support a model where histone acetylation-dependent spatiotemporal dynamics of BRD2 coordinate the transcription machinery to regulate transcription initiation.
